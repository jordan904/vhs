## Idea 1: Maritime Craftsman Aesthetic

**Design Movement**: Arts & Crafts Revival meets Coastal Industrial

**Core Principles**:
1. Honest materials - celebrate the natural wood grain and metal textures visible in the portfolio
2. Structural clarity - layouts that mirror the precision of carpentry with clear visual hierarchy
3. Weathered authenticity - embrace the rugged Nova Scotia coastal character
4. Functional beauty - every element serves a purpose, no decorative excess

**Color Philosophy**:
- **Primary**: Deep navy (#1a365d) - represents the Atlantic, trust, and the blue roof in the logo
- **Secondary**: Warm cedar (#8B5A2B) - reflects the natural wood used in their projects
- **Accent**: Weathered steel grey (#6B7280) - metal roofing reference
- **Background**: Warm off-white (#FAF8F5) - like aged paper or natural canvas
- **Emotional intent**: Trustworthy, grounded, locally rooted

**Layout Paradigm**: 
- Asymmetric grid with intentional negative space
- Content blocks positioned like building materials - some stacked, some offset
- Full-bleed images alternating with contained text sections
- Diagonal dividers echoing roof lines

**Signature Elements**:
1. Diagonal section dividers mimicking roof pitch angles
2. Subtle wood grain texture overlays on accent sections
3. Blueprint-style decorative lines and corner marks

**Interaction Philosophy**:
- Interactions feel substantial and deliberate, like placing a beam
- Hover states reveal depth through shadow, not color change
- Form inputs have visible "construction" states

**Animation**:
- Sections slide in from angles matching roof pitch (12-15 degrees)
- Images reveal with a horizontal wipe like a saw cut
- Numbers count up with a satisfying mechanical feel
- Subtle parallax on hero images suggesting depth and craftsmanship

**Typography System**:
- **Display**: Playfair Display (serif) - craftsmanship, established
- **Body**: Source Sans 3 - clean, readable, professional
- **Accent**: Oswald (condensed) - for labels and CTAs

## Idea 2: Nova Scotia Modern Vernacular

**Design Movement**: Scandinavian Minimalism meets Atlantic Canadian Vernacular

**Core Principles**:
1. Light-filled clarity - reflecting the bright Nova Scotia skies
2. Purposeful restraint - let the project photos speak
3. Warm minimalism - clean but not cold
4. Local authenticity - subtle nods to Maritime heritage

**Color Philosophy**:
- **Primary**: Ocean blue (#2563EB) - bright, optimistic, matching logo
- **Secondary**: Driftwood grey (#9CA3AF) - weathered, natural
- **Accent**: Sunset amber (#F59E0B) - warmth, energy, call-to-action
- **Background**: Cool white (#FAFBFC) with warm grey sections (#F3F4F6)
- **Emotional intent**: Fresh, capable, approachable, modern

**Layout Paradigm**:
- Clean horizontal bands with generous whitespace
- Left-aligned content creating a strong vertical axis
- Card-based service displays with consistent proportions
- Sticky navigation with progressive reveal

**Signature Elements**:
1. Soft rounded corners (8-12px) suggesting approachability
2. Subtle gradient overlays on hero images (blue to transparent)
3. Thin accent lines as section separators

**Interaction Philosophy**:
- Light, responsive interactions that feel effortless
- Micro-animations provide feedback without delay
- Forms guide users with clear progressive disclosure

**Animation**:
- Elements fade up gently (200ms, ease-out)
- Cards lift slightly on hover with soft shadow expansion
- Page transitions use cross-fade for continuity
- Scroll-triggered reveals are subtle, not dramatic

**Typography System**:
- **Display**: DM Sans (bold) - modern, geometric, friendly
- **Body**: DM Sans (regular) - consistency and readability
- **Accent**: DM Sans (medium) - unified type family

## Idea 3: Rugged Atlantic Industrial

**Design Movement**: Industrial Brutalism meets Maritime Functionality

**Core Principles**:
1. Bold confidence - unapologetic strength reflecting construction work
2. Material honesty - exposed structure, visible grid
3. High contrast - clear hierarchy, no ambiguity
4. Weather-tested durability - design that feels built to last

**Color Philosophy**:
- **Primary**: Steel blue (#1E3A5F) - deep, authoritative, logo-aligned
- **Secondary**: Concrete grey (#4B5563) - industrial, substantial
- **Accent**: Safety orange (#EA580C) - high visibility CTAs
- **Background**: Near-black (#111827) with light sections (#F9FAFB)
- **Emotional intent**: Powerful, reliable, no-nonsense, professional

- Bold asymmetric compositions with dramatic scale contrast
- Oversized typography as structural element
- Split-screen layouts dividing image and content
- Visible grid lines as design elements

**Signature Elements**:
1. Exposed grid lines and construction marks
2. Angled text blocks cutting across images
3. Bold geometric shapes as accent elements

- **Display**: Bebas Neue - industrial, impactful, bold

## Selected Design: Maritime Craftsman Aesthetic (Idea 1)

I'm selecting the **Maritime Craftsman Aesthetic** because it best represents:
- The quality craftsmanship visible in the portfolio photos
- The Nova Scotia coastal identity
- A professional yet approachable feel for homeowners
- Strong alignment with the blue roof logo and brand colors
- Warmth and trust that encourages conversion

This design will feature diagonal roof-line dividers, warm wood tones paired with deep navy, and typography that feels established and trustworthy while remaining modern and readable.
